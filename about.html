About Us

Nestled in the shadow of the historic ruins, The Corfe Castle Bakery has been filling the air of Corfe Castle with the aroma of caramelized sugar and warm butter for over three centuries. Founded in 1712, this beloved bakery is famous for its signature hearth-baked sourdough and intricately layered pastries, all crafted using traditional stone-milled Dorset flour. Visitors and locals alike flock to our cozy stone cottage shop to experience a true taste of Purbeck heritage, where every loaf is shaped by hand and baked to a perfect, golden crunch.

The Bakery's History

But the true secret to our legendary crusts lies in our whimsical history. Local lore tells of Ignis, a gentle, smoke-grey dragon who nested in the castle ruins and became the bakery's most treasured employee during the bitter winter of 1745. Tired of freezing in the drafty keep, Ignis struck a deal with the master baker: in exchange for a daily ration of sweet currant buns, he would use his perfectly regulated, smoke-free flame to heat the massive brick ovens to the exact temperatures required for delicate pastries. Today, while our ovens are a bit more modern, we still honor our fiery founder's legacy by dusting our famous "Dragon-Breath Buns" with a distinct, warming pinch of cinnamon and cardamom
